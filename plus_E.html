What is the connection between Bitcoin and speculation ?

How to calculate the value ?

Bitcoin is a currency, a crypto-currency, but the calculation of its value is not very different from other currencies or scarce goods. Indeed its value changes according to supply and demand. Its demand must be higher than its supply for its price to be high. Speculation therefore plays a key role in its value.

What is speculation ?

Speculation is the act of buying an asset with a view to making a profit from its subsequent resale. To explain it is like making a bet on an object, on an event that may or may not happen. You have bought your bet (object, event). If your bet wins and you have guessed what will happen then your asset will be worth more than when you bought it. The difference in price between when you bought it and the current price is the money you have made or lost if you sell it.
The difference between items that may have a purely factual value, a utility - the vast majority of bitcoin buyers do not buy it for a utilitarian purpose.

What is speculation in bitcoin?

Bitcoin and its development is mainly based on speculation, it is its main engine. Its price indeed varies according to the traders' assessment of its future (the money that can be made on the currency).

Why is it so attractive to speculators ?

At first glance one might wonder why so many people invest in bitcoin, it is not pegged to a currency or gold and does not respond to any stock exchange organization. These features make it attractive because it is based on a new technology: the Blockchain. There are many blockchains, which are computer systems that can be accessed anywhere in the world. They allow the storage and exchange of value on the Internet without a centralized intermediary. They store bitcoins in the form of blockchains. Since bitcoins are complex strings of numbers, they store strings of numbers. People with a bitcoin wallet can sell bitcoins to each other. What makes the blockchain special is its control mode: anyone with a wallet can validate or not an operation of the blockchain (change, modification of a block) and its modifications are registered on a kind of register. This register can be accessed at any time by all these people.

It is this transparency that makes bitcoin so attractive.

Risks of speculation. ct, bitcoin does not escape its risks. Indeed, there can be speculative bubbles (a sustained and self-sustaining increase in the gap between the price and the fundamental value of an asset) which can lead to crashes (a sudden drop in the price of an asset class). Bitcoin has experienced several crashes, notably in April 2021. At that time it lost almost 30% of its value and even went as low as half its value. What are the reasons for this crash? It is important to understand that even if bitcoin is independent and based on the blockchain, its value is not based on speculation. Indeed, it depends on real monetary policies, mainstream acceptance and media coverage. In 2021 the crash was partly due to a tweet from Elon Musk announcing that Tesla would no longer accept bitcoins because of its environmental cost, but also because Chinese monetary authorities had highlighted its speculative nature.

Conclusion

To conclude, bitcoin is a recent currency, its value is largely based on speculation. This speculation is due to a particular and attractive operating mode: the blockchain. However, this attractiveness makes it dangerous because of its speculative nature.

Has Bitcoin become part of the global economy?

The beginnings of Bitcoin in the economy

Since its inception in 2009, bitcoin has grown steadily, operating admirably and attracting millions of users. This crypto-currency, conceived in the midst of the crisis, was initially intended to be a currency free of state and banking influence. Today, this multifaceted and evolving system has the potential to revolutionize many activities, such as the financial system or currency, and by extension states. In the global economy, its impact is important, but two visions oppose each other regarding its benefits and its future.

Bitcoin according to the optimists

First of all, according to the most optimistic, the crypto-currency industry is growing faster and faster, and will give wonderful economic growth opportunities to its owners. Indeed, bitcoin has already allowed many people and businesses to grow and evolve, relying mainly on the exchange.

Towards simplification?

In addition, crypto-currencies and blockchain technology allow entrepreneurs around the world to receive international quotes and from any location, with this new technology contributing to a new financial development. In addition, many crypto-currency services facilitate financial transactions, especially with international partners and investors.

What are the goals of the Bitcoin project ?

The purpose of bitcoin would then be to help and grow small and medium sized businesses around the world, in order to get better financial assistance and more flexible relational stability between different international businesses. With these new, fully digitized wallets, an entrepreneur might be able to quickly and efficiently convert these crypto-currencies into fiat currency and, through this, reinvest those profits back into their business. On the other hand, bitcoin (and all other digital currencies) will likely bring international economic growth and increased financial investment worldwide. Thousands or millions of people will be able to use, invest, convert and manage their savings with bitcoin from anywhere in the world.

Bitcoin a speculative bubble ?

In history, many currencies have made their mark, such as the "pound" or the "dollar," but in the case of bitcoin, its scale and ease of international use favor the use of this digital currency. In addition, some central banks support this currency and are considering using it in a practical
However, many people are more wary of bitcoin. Indeed, despite the financial normalization of bitcoin, made credible by the futures contracts launched by the Chicago Stock Exchange, the volatility of the value of bitcoin makes it a very particular financial asset. It is therefore difficult to see this value as anything other than a financial bubble.

A not so perfect currency ?

Moreover, even if they have not been detected or exploited to date, flaws in the blockchain protocol are theoretically possible. There is no doubt that many hackers are currently trying to discover them. Without going to this extreme risk, which would open the door to abuses still difficult to imagine, the attraction of hackers for crypto-currencies is increasingly concrete.

Bitcoin's limits ?

Less known but not anecdotal at a time when energy consumption poses major problems, the energy drain represented by the operation of the blockchain and bitcoins poses real problems. On the investor side, the main risks are undoubtedly related to governance. The quality of the latter will have a direct impact on the confidence of economic agents. This trust is essential for the development of a currency that is still used exclusively by early adopters, and that probably does not reflect the behavior of the majority of players.

Has the end of Bitcoin begun?

Regulation will surely accompany an awareness of the excesses and dangers associated with the development of bitcoins. Political actors and central banks undoubtedly have major assets to quickly curb a phenomenon that could escape them if they wait too long. Beyond limiting the use of bitcoins to specific sectors, which is difficult to put in place, alternative currencies could emerge. This is already the case, and political actors could be tempted to encourage their emergence, using every conceivable lever, in order to reduce the role of bitcoin, or even make it obsolete.

Conclusion

The development of bitcoin, which is difficult to control and excessive, constitutes a threat to the economy, like all speculative bubbles. Despite the new characteristics of the product and the lack of experience of the actors likely to play the role of regulators, the development of bitcoin should be limited by the relativity of the trust that will be placed in it by the economic actors.
